## Selecting the Right Stone for Each Space

Choosing the ideal stone requires both aesthetic vision and technical understanding. Here’s practical guidance for making the best selection:

- **Kitchen Areas**: Opt for dense, low-porosity stones like granite or engineered quartz for kitchen stone countertops. These materials resist staining and withstand daily use.
- **Bathrooms**: Marble bathroom design remains a hallmark of luxury. Consider sealed marble or limestone for a spa-like feel, taking into account required maintenance.
- **Living Spaces**: For exclusive stone interiors, travertine and sandstone offer soft textures, while bespoke stone furniture crafted from rare stones creates statement pieces.
- **Outdoor Zones**: Weather-resistant stones such as slate or basalt enhance terraces and garden features, ensuring longevity in Northern European climates.

## Care and Maintenance Tips for Lasting Beauty

To maintain the pristine look of your stone surfaces:

- Always use pH-neutral cleaners and avoid harsh chemicals.
- Seal stone surfaces regularly as recommended for each material.
- Wipe spills promptly, especially on marble, to prevent staining.
- Use mats and pads under hot dishes or heavy objects to protect countertops.
