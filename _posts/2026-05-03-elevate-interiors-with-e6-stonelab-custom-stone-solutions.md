## Current Trends in Stone Interior Design

The e6 trend is all about personal expression and sustainable luxury. Here are some of the leading directions in exclusive stone interiors:

- **Natural Finishes**: Honed, matte, and textured surfaces are replacing high-gloss looks, highlighting stone’s organic beauty.
- **Monochrome Palettes**: Subtle shades of grey, beige, and creamy white are favored for their calming, understated luxury.
- **Seamless Integration**: Stone is increasingly used for continuous surfaces—think matching countertops, backsplashes, and integrated sinks in kitchens, or floor-to-wall marble bathroom designs.
- **Bespoke Stone Furniture**: Custom tables, shelving, and even lighting crafted from natural stone are becoming signature pieces in high-end homes.
- **Sustainable Choices**: Clients are seeking stones with transparent provenance, recycled content, and minimal environmental footprint.

## Selecting the Right Stone for Each Space

Choosing the perfect stone involves balancing aesthetics, function, and longevity. Here are some expert tips from the team at StoneLab:

- **Kitchen Countertops**: Select durable stones such as granite or engineered quartz for areas with heavy use. Look for finishes that resist stains and scratches, and consider lighter shades to create an airy, modern feel.
- **Bathrooms**: Marble remains a favorite for its classic elegance, especially in bathrooms. Choose sealed, slip-resistant finishes for floors and consider large-format slabs for a seamless, spa-like effect.
- **Living Spaces**: Statement fireplaces, feature walls, or bespoke stone furniture can become the focal point of living rooms. Opt for unique veining and custom shapes for a truly exclusive touch.
- **Entryways and Hallways**: Durable, low-maintenance stones like limestone or terrazzo set the tone for luxury from the moment guests arrive.

## Care and Maintenance Tips for Lasting Beauty

Stone surfaces are remarkably resilient, but a few simple practices will keep them looking pristine for decades:

- **Regular Cleaning**: Use a pH-neutral cleaner and a soft cloth for daily maintenance. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive pads.
- **Sealing**: Periodically seal natural stone surfaces to protect against staining—especially in kitchens and bathrooms.
- **Prompt Spill Cleanup**: Wipe up spills—especially acidic substances like wine, coffee, or citrus—immediately to prevent etching.
- **Professional Restoration**: For scratches or dullness, trust professional stone craftsmen to restore the surface’s original luster.
