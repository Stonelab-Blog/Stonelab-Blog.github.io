### Current Trends in Stone Interiors Across Scandinavia and Northern Europe

Across Sweden, Northern Europe, Germany, and the UK, several key trends are redefining exclusive stone interiors:

- **Soft, neutral palettes**: Earthy tones and subtle veins are favored for their calming, sophisticated effect.
- **Textured finishes**: Honed, leathered, or sandblasted surfaces add depth and tactile interest, particularly in kitchen stone countertops and bathroom vanities.
- **Large-format stone slabs**: These create seamless, dramatic installations in open-plan spaces, enhancing a sense of flow.
- **Integrated lighting**: Backlit stone and under-counter illumination highlight the natural beauty of the material, especially in bespoke stone furniture.

### Choosing the Right Stone for Every Space in Sweden and Beyond

Selecting the ideal stone involves more than aesthetics—it’s about matching material properties to the demands of each room.

- **Kitchens**: For countertops, opt for durable stones such as granite or engineered quartz that withstand daily use and are easy to maintain.
- **Bathrooms**: Marble and limestone deliver timeless beauty, while StoneLab’s advanced treatments ensure longevity in wet environments.
- **Living areas**: Statement fireplace surrounds or bespoke stone furniture pieces showcase personality while anchoring the design.
- **Outdoor spaces**: Weather-resistant stones extend luxury to terraces and gardens, blending seamlessly with Scandinavian landscapes.

### Caring for Your Stone Surfaces: Expert Tips from StoneLab

To preserve the beauty of your exclusive stone interiors, follow these expert tips:

- **Routine cleaning**: Wipe surfaces with a soft cloth and pH-neutral cleaner to prevent damage.
- **Seal regularly**: Natural stone benefits from periodic sealing, especially in high-traffic or wet areas.
- **Avoid harsh chemicals**: Steer clear of acidic or abrasive cleaners that can etch or dull finishes.
- **Immediate attention**: Quickly clean spills, particularly from acidic substances like wine or citrus, to prevent staining.
