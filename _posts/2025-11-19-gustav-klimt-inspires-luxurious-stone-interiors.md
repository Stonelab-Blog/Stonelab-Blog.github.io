### Current Style Trends in Luxury Interior Stone Design: Klimt’s Influence Reimagined

Modern luxury interiors are moving beyond minimalism, embracing bold materials and sophisticated detailing. Inspired by Klimt’s golden accents and organic motifs, designers in Sweden, Germany, and the UK are choosing:

- **Richly veined marbles** for statement kitchen islands and bathroom vanities  
- **Bespoke stone furniture** with inlaid metallic or colored stone details  
- **Textured wall panels** and feature fireplaces, echoing the layered artistry of Klimt’s works  

This fusion of art and Swedish stone craftsmanship brings a unique identity to each space, turning surfaces into focal points.

### Choosing the Perfect Stone for Each Space in Your Luxury Home or Project

Selecting the right stone is crucial to achieving a cohesive and luxurious atmosphere. Here are a few practical guidelines tailored for luxury homeowners, interior designers, architects, and developers in Northern Europe:

- **Kitchens:** Opt for durable, low-porosity stones like granite or engineered quartz for kitchen stone countertops. These materials resist staining and scratching while radiating elegance.  
- **Bathrooms:** Marble bathroom design remains a classic choice, but consider honed finishes for a softer, matte look that minimizes water spots.  
- **Living Spaces:** For bespoke stone furniture and accent walls, select stones with unique patterns and colors to create visual interest without overwhelming the space.

**Tip:** Always request samples from your stone supplier—like StoneLab—to see how the stone interacts with your lighting and other materials.

### Care and Maintenance for Lasting Beauty of Luxury Interior Materials

Luxury interior materials deserve attentive care. Maintain your stone surfaces with these recommendations:

- **Daily cleaning:** Use a mild, pH-neutral cleaner and a soft cloth. Avoid acidic substances that could etch the stone.  
- **Sealing:** Many stones benefit from regular sealing to protect against stains—especially in kitchens and bathrooms.  
- **Avoid abrasives:** Do not use harsh sponges or powders, which can dull the finish.
